TV Energy Insights: Data Story

The visualisations reflect patterns in Australian television energy performance and availability to guide consumer decision-making.

1. What type of TV screen technologies are currently available in Australia and which are the most frequent?

Most TVs in Australia use LCD technology (often marketed as LED TVs), with OLED models making up a much smaller share.

2. What screen sizes are currently available, and which are the most frequent?

Screens around 55 inches are the most common, followed by other medium and large sizes like 65 and 75 inches, while smaller screens appear less often.

3. Which brands have the greatest number of different models?

Samsung, Kogan, and LG offer the widest range of TV models, with brands like Hisense, Philips, Sony, and TCL also having a noticeable presence.

4. Which type of screen technology consumes the least amount of power?

LCD screens use the least power overall, followed by LED-backlit LCDs and then OLED models.

5. What is the relationship between screen size and power use?

Larger TVs generally use more power than smaller ones.

6. What is the relationship between star rating and screen size?

Energy star ratings are spread across all screen sizes, and even larger TVs can still be energy efficient.

Conclusion

The market shows plenty of choice, with 55-inch LCD models standing out as the most common and efficient, while larger sizes and other technologies remain available for different needs.
